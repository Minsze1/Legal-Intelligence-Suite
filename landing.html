What it does

Four tools. One goal.

Every feature is built around a single outcome: getting your child home as fast as the law allows.

📄
Appellate Analyzer
Upload your appeal motion. Docket Watch identifies every structural defect and dismissal risk, then drafts a conformed revised motion optimized for reversal — with argument order set to the fastest path to your child coming home. A final QC pass checks every technicality before you file.
Structural Analysis Likely Ruling First Impression Conform Final QC
⚖️
District Analyzer
Upload any district court order, memorandum, or journal entry. The analyzer deconstructs every adverse ruling, checks all statutory requirements, runs K.S.A. 60-260(b) void judgment grounds, and drafts a complete vacate or alter-and-amend motion ready to file.
Order Summary Due Process 60-260(b) CINC Checklist Vacate Framework
🧪
Theory Lab
Propose any legal theory. The Lab evaluates it against the Kansas CINC framework, checks whether it's already in your motion, researches unpublished and recent opinions, and — if the theory is beneficial — prompts you to add it with the statutory basis and on-point published case law to review first.
Framework Fit Deep Research Saved List Transfer to Motion
📝
Motion Creator
Upload a district court order and the Motion Creator selects the correct response motion type, drafts a filing-ready motion with caption, numbered arguments, verification, and certificate of service — then runs a final QC pass with a plain-English summary explaining what the motion argues and what to expect.
Motion Selection Draft Motion Theory Integration Strategic Notes
How it works

Upload. Analyze. File.

No account required. No forms to fill out. Upload your document and the suite goes to work.

1
Upload your document
Drop in any court order, appeal motion, or memorandum as a PDF, Word document, or text file. The suite reads it in full.
2
Get the full analysis
Every defect, every missing statutory finding, every due process violation — organized into clear sections you can read without a law degree. Plain-English summaries explain what it means for your case.
3
Conform, QC, download
Produce a filing-ready draft, then run a final QC pass that checks every technicality and dismissal risk. Download, review with an attorney if possible, and file.
Who it's for

Built for people who can't afford to lose.

The child welfare system is complex by design. Docket Watch levels the information gap.

Pro se parents
Navigating a CINC case without an attorney is one of the hardest things a parent can do. Docket Watch gives you the same statutory analysis and motion-drafting capacity that a CINC attorney would use — without the hourly rate.
Legal aid organizations
High-volume family defense work means stretched attorneys and limited research time. Docket Watch accelerates first-pass analysis, surfaces dismissal risks early, and produces draft motions attorneys can review and refine rather than build from scratch.
Family advocates & social workers
Understanding what the agency is required to do — 14-day notice, mandatory prerequisites, three-part TPR test — helps advocates support families more effectively and catch agency failures before they become permanent outcomes.

Your child deserves every argument the law allows.

Docket Watch is a research and drafting tool — not a substitute for legal counsel. But it gives you the knowledge to fight informed, file correctly, and know your rights.
